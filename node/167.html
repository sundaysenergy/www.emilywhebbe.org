Birthday "cupcakes"

August 3, 2012 - 10:43am — Emily Whebbe

I searched high and low for a good recipe that I felt comfortable feeding Reverie for her first birthday. I wanted to avoid sugar, grains, oils, eggs, and dairy. Usually, cupcakes are kind of a difficult thing to make without any of the above. Then I remembered coconut flour. It's a great non-grain to use and full of fiber and protein. I substitute it in many of my recipes calling for whole wheat flour (as they have similar "grainy" texture), but the only quip is that it requires a lot of eggs or egg-like foods to bind it together as it doesn't have the glutenous properties grain flours have. So, instead of using eggs, I added bananas and flax hoping they'd keep things together like eggs usually do. They did! So, without boring you more with kitchen chemistry, here is the recipe:

Ingredients:
5 smashed bananas
4 tablespoons ground flaxseed "soaked" in 12 tablespoons of water
2 cups applesauce
5 tablespoons molasses
1/2 cup coconut oil
1 cup raw coconut flour (I get mine here)
1/4 cup cinnamon
cloves, ginger, nutmeg (or whatever other spices you want in whatever amounts you like)
2 tsp baking soda
2 tsp vanilla

Soak the flaxseed meal in water. Mix all ingredients except the flax in a bowl with a wooden spoon or immersion blender. Add flaxseed meal after mixing all the other ingredients. Pour into muffin tins and top with an apple slice or banana slice for garnish (optional). Likewise you can put whatever fruit pieces you like on the top.

Bake at 350 degrees in muffin tins (or 325 if you're using glass or stoneware "tins"). Don't overbake as they'll dry out!

That's it! You have gluten, egg, sugar, and dairy-free cupcakes!
