Coconut Berry Banana Muffins

June 20, 2012 - 9:46am — Emily Whebbe

I came across this recipe from PBS the other day and figured I'd try it since I had a ton of bananas to cook up. Of course, I modified it, but only a bit so I'm giving them credit still. It works great for all those garden berries you might have!

Ingredients:

1 cup brown sugar, packed
1/4 cup coconut oil
1/4 cup butter (I always use salted butter so I don't have to add salt)
2 eggs
1 tablespoon flaxseed meal soaked in 3 tablespoons water
4 mashed bananas
2 cups whole wheat flour
1 tsp baking soda
1/2 cup coconut flakes (sweetened or unsweetened, you choose)
Berries: blueberries, raspberries, mulberries, currants, blackberries (although baking muffins with blackberries is a little tricky due to their size), anything!

Prepare muffin tin and preheat oven to 350 degrees. Mix sugar, oils, eggs, flax, and bananas. Add flour and baking soda and mix only until combined. Add berries. Pour in tins and bake. If you like, add a sliced strawberry or banana atop each muffin.

You can substitute the butter for another 1/4 cup coconut oil but I ran out of coconut oil when I baked these so I used butter. Likewise, you can use 1/2 cup butter and omit the coconut oil entirely if you don't have any.
