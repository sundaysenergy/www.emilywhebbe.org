Spelt Pizza

April 14, 2012 - 8:39am — Emily Whebbe

Having an infant doesn't really yield to having your hands in dough very long. So, I tried to think of different ways to make dough easily, with little prep time. I came across the following recipe (which I of course modified a little). When I told Kai I was making spelt crust pizza he said "That's really difficult." I informed him I had a secret ingredient (Ricotta cheese) but still I had my own reservations if it would actually be good. Two words: it is. Make it. Eat it. Enjoy it.

2 cups whole grain spelt flour
2 teaspoons baking powder
1/2 teaspoon sea salt
1 teaspoon sugar
1 cup whole-milk ricotta cheese
1/4 cup whole milk
2 tablespoons linseed oil or extra-virgin olive oil
1 egg
Coarse cornmeal

Mix dry and wet separately and add together, until it becomes a nice tacky dough. Kneed a few times (5-7 turns) and mold into a ball. Cover and let sit for at least an hour (I left it overnight in the fridge so the grains soften quite nicely). When ready to use it, divide into 2 (or leave it whole for a large pizza) and kneed a few times on a floured surface. No need to kneed very much, just about 10 turns. Roll out and use. Top with whatever you like.

Place on a pizza stone that is well heated to 425 degrees and coat the bottom of the pizza with cornmeal to make it easily transferrable from the oven to the pizza peel.
