Quinoa Chocolate Cake (Gluten-Free)

July 13, 2012 - 2:49pm — Emily Whebbe

I started off my birthday weekend by making this cake into cupcakes for breakfast...which turned into having them for lunch as well as "snack."

I came across this recipe a few weeks ago and made it for our July 4th celebration. I liked it so much I pre-made a mix with the dry ingredients and froze some extra quinoa in hopes it would make it even easier to make in the future. I didn't even have it stored for 10 days before wanting to make it again. So, here's the recipe:

4 eggs
1/3 cup whole milk
3/4 cup coconut oil (melted and cooled, or just room temperature if it's 75 degrees or hotter in your house!)
1 teaspoon vanilla
1 mashed banana
2 cups cooked quinoa, cooled (I cooked 1 cup quinoa in 2 cups water and that made plenty more than the 2 cups this recipe called for)

1/2 cup almond flour/meal
1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
1 cup sugar/honey/sweetener of your choice (you can add more sugar if you want)
1.5 teaspoons baking powder (aluminum-free)
1/2 teaspoon baking soda
chocolate chips to top (if making into cupcakes)

Directions: Put eggs, coconut oil, milk, vanilla, and banana in blender and mix on medium for 1 minute. Add cooked quinoa and blend until smooth (be careful not to over blend if you have a Vitamix like I do!). In a separate bowl, mix dry ingredients. Add blender mixture to dry ingredients being careful not to over-mix. Pour into muffin tins or cake pan(s) and bake at 350 degrees. If you're making this into a cake, I would double the recipe for a 9" x 13" pan. Otherwise it will make two 8" cakes quite nicely, but they'll be short.

Enjoy.
