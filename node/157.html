Better than Paula Deen's Bread Pudding

February 7, 2012 - 5:45pm — Emily Whebbe

A few weeks ago Paula Deen confirmed rumors that she has Type II Diabetes. So, in light of her famous recipes that often bulge with sugar, I decided to use some old bread we had laying around and make her bread pudding.

At first, I vowed to follow the recipe (like I never do), hoping it wouldn't be too sweet. I couldn't. I simply couldn't add as much sugar as she called for...and I was doubling the recipe. I hate to say it, I'm sure she's a wonderful woman who comforts herself and many others with her sweet creations, but I'll take my version:

Bread Pudding

8 cups day-old bread: cubed
8 eggs
4 cups coconut milk (I used the kind that you buy in the refrigerator section at the store, not the coconut milk in a can)
1 T. vanilla
1 T. cinnamon
1 tsp nutmeg
1/2 tsp cloves
freshly grated ginger (to desired amount)
1/4 cup butter
1 cup sugar or honey
Raisins
1/2 cup Rum/brandy/other liquor

Cube bread and put in 9" x 13" greased pan. Sprinkle raisins (can be soaked in liquor overnight) throughout bread. Beat eggs and add milk, sugar, spices, and vanilla. Pour over bread and let sit 5-10 minutes. Melt butter and mix liquor into it. Pour over soaked bread, making sure all bread is coated with the egg mixture. Bake at 350 degrees for 45-60 minutes.

For a sauce, melt 1/2 cup butter (or more) and add a few tablespoons of brown sugar. If you like, you can also add more liquor to this mixture. Serve warm.
