Homemade Granola

September 14, 2012 - 3:51pm — Emily Whebbe

Fall seems like a good time to set aside some time to make granola. I have discovered that most cereals and granolas contain some sort of vegetable oil and I'm tired of eating that. I also can't really afford "good" granola/cereals as they cost upwards of $6-8 a pound. Making my own seemed like a cheaper, healthier alternative, and also something so easy, I don't know why I haven't been doing it longer. I added pumpkin seeds and cranberries to this recipe, to match the season, but you could add whatever flavors you like.

Ingredients:

6 cups rolled oats
3-6 tablespoons brown sugar (depending on how sugary you like your granola)
dash of salt
cinnamon
2 tsp vanilla
1/2 cup coconut oil
1/2 cup honey
2 tablespoons ginger spread (or grated ginger)
nuts
dried fruit

Preheat oven to 250 degrees. Mix oats, sugar, salt, cinnamon (and any other spices you may like) in a bowl. In a separate bowl mix the coconut oil, honey, vanilla, and ginger spread. Pour honey mixture over oats and make sure everything is coated evenly. Spread granola in a 11"x14" pan or cookie sheet with sides. I use a glass casserole dish that works just as well. (Note: you'll need two pans probably, or just make half the batch later). Bake for 15 minutes and stir the granola. Then continue baking another 15-25 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes or so. Granola will be soft when it comes out of the oven but will harden as it cools. Once cooled, add nuts, seeds, dried fruit, or anything you wish and mix together. And finally, Eat!
