Everyday Cookies

August 25, 2012 - 4:12pm — Emily Whebbe

Everyday cookies. You know, the kind you want to eat all day, every day. The kind that you wish you could make enough batter of to freeze, but by the time you start baking the dough you think, "Why not just bake it all so there are plenty of them for me to EAT?" Well, this recipe is for that kind of cookie. They have a taste that's timeless, an ease that's priceless, and are fit for a bottomless stomach. If you can find the strength, the dough freezes well and can be stored for up to 6 months (or longer if you have a deep freeze and if you're substituting the eggs)

Ingredients:

1 cup coconut oil or butter
2 cups almond flour
2 cups white or whole wheat flour
cinnamon and nutmeg to taste
grated ginger to taste
1/2 to 1 cup maple syrup, or other sweetener of your choice
1 teaspoon baking soda salt (if using unsalted butter or coconut oil)
5 teaspoons vanilla
2 eggs
raisins
dark chocolate chips

Directions: Mix almond flour, grain flour, baking soda together. Soften butter or coconut oil and add to it the vanilla and maple syrup. Add to dry ingredients and mix well. Add eggs and dough should form. Add raisins and chocolate chips. As you're forming them into cookies, press the dough together to make sure they don't crumble when they're done. Bake at 350 for 10 minutes or so. Do not over bake!
