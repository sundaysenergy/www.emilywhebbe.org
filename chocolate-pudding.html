Chocolate Pudding

May 1, 2013 - 2:47pm — Emily Whebbe

Oh dang, I'm on a roll with what shall be called "recipes that need a quotation mark," or "foods that would never be sold to the mainstream public," or "yes I'm really that much of a hippy that I made this delicious not-cake/not-pudding/sugar, grain, dairy-free food for you!" Yes, I'm on a downhill (but really uphill) slide (but really a ladder) into the depths (but really heights) of healthful cooking.

This isn't really a departure from before (read my post on Rev's birthday cupcakes), but it's definitely becoming more of an obsession lately as I've decided to go sugar and most grains free (I'm still eating honey and my jar that I opened two days ago is already half gone). I'm convinced the Beez Kneez will save me. It's by far my favorite local honey to date.

The following recipe is great for a quick, chocolate treat, and appends nicely to my $20+ a week avocado budget. I got the idea from a friend and tweaked it a bit, as I do with most things.

Ingredients:

1-2 cups ripe avocado
2 tablespoons sunflower nut butter
2 tablespoons raw honey (or more if you like)
1/2 cup cocoa powder
2 tablespoons coconut milk
Ginger
Cinnamon

Blend all ingredients in a blender or vitamix and enjoy!

I actually made this into the filling for a raw pie. I used pecans and raisins (what I had laying around) for the crust, with a tad of cardamom, cinnamon, nutmeg, and a few tablespoons of coconut oil blended together. I then added a little bit of cream cheese and honey softened to the chocolate pudding mixture to make it more like a cheesecake filling. Assembled and stored in the fridge, it won't last long.
